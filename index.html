What is Psychodrama?

Psychodrama is an experiential form of therapy that uses guided role-playing, dramatic enactment, and group dynamics to help individuals express and process emotions. By stepping into different roles and reliving significant life experiences, participants can gain profound insights into their emotions, behaviors, and relationships. This method encourages self-discovery, improves interpersonal skills, and fosters emotional healing, while promoting empathy, self-awareness, and emotional release.
